### __How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?__  
User stories help me cut through the ambiguity for these requests. They take vague desires and turn them into clear, tangible pieces. During this project the user stories gave me a way to check whether a feature was actually useful or just another addition that'll bog down the team. When the client changed their request, the updated stories gave me a clean path forward.

### __How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?__  
I work iteratively, in bite size chunks. Agile fits well how I think. Sprints give me structure without locking me into bad decisions. Daily standups, the retros, and the planning sessions all serve different purposes, but they work together to keep things moving. I plan to keep using those tools even if it's just me working solo. They help me keep focus and adjust when things change.

### __What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?__  
Being a good team member means doing your part, communicating clearly, and not making things harder for others. Sometimes that’s stepping up and taking over or helping, sometimes it will require stepping back and letting someone else take point. Teamwork is not about being perfect, it’s about being reliable and making sure the team keeps moving forward no matter what.
